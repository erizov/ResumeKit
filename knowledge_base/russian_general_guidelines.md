# Russian Resume Guidelines - General Market

## Overview

Russian resumes (CV) follow specific conventions that differ significantly from Western markets. Understanding these is crucial for successful job applications in Russia.

## Key Characteristics

### 1. Personal Information
- **Photo**: Professional photo is standard (top-right corner)
- **Personal Details**: Include date of birth, marital status (optional), citizenship
- **Contact**: Full address, phone, email, social media (LinkedIn, Telegram)

### 2. Structure and Order

**Standard Order:**
1. Personal Information (with photo)
2. Objective/Summary (Цель)
3. Work Experience (Опыт работы) - Most recent first
4. Education (Образование)
5. Skills (Навыки)
6. Languages (Языки)
7. Additional Information (Дополнительная информация)
8. References (Рекомендации) - Optional

### 3. Length
- **Standard**: 2-3 pages for experienced professionals
- **Entry-level**: 1-2 pages acceptable
- More detail is expected compared to US market

### 4. Style and Tone

**Formal Tone:**
- Use formal language (Вы instead of ты)
- Professional, respectful tone
- Avoid overly casual expressions

**Formatting:**
- Clear section headers
- Consistent formatting
- Professional fonts (Times New Roman, Arial)
- Font size: 11-12pt

### 5. Work Experience

**Format:**
- Company name, position, dates (month/year)
- Detailed responsibilities (5-8 bullet points per role)
- Achievements with metrics when possible
- Technologies and tools used

**Example Structure:**
```
Компания: ООО "ТехноСофт"
Должность: Backend Developer
Период: Январь 2020 - Настоящее время

Обязанности:
- Разработка серверной части веб-приложений на Python/FastAPI
- Оптимизация производительности (улучшение на 40%)
- Работа с базами данных PostgreSQL, MongoDB
```

### 6. Education

**Include:**
- University name, degree, specialization
- Graduation year
- Relevant coursework or thesis topic (if applicable)
- Academic achievements (optional)

### 7. Skills Section

**Organization:**
- Group by category (Programming Languages, Frameworks, Tools)
- List proficiency level (Базовый, Средний, Продвинутый)
- Include both hard and soft skills

### 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too Short**: Russian employers expect detailed resumes
2. **Missing Photo**: Professional photo is expected
3. **Informal Tone**: Maintain professional, formal language
4. **Vague Descriptions**: Be specific about responsibilities and achievements
5. **No Metrics**: Include quantifiable achievements when possible

### 9. ATS Considerations

**For Russian ATS Systems:**
- Use standard section headers
- Include relevant keywords from job description
- Maintain clean formatting (avoid complex tables)
- Use standard date formats (ММ.ГГГГ)

### 10. Cultural Notes

- **Stability Valued**: Long tenure at companies is viewed positively
- **Education Important**: University education is highly valued
- **Certifications**: Professional certifications are appreciated
- **References**: May be requested, have them ready

## Best Practices Summary

1. Include professional photo
2. Use formal, professional tone
3. Provide detailed work experience descriptions
4. Include all relevant personal information
5. Maintain 2-3 page length for experienced candidates
6. Use clear section headers
7. Include quantifiable achievements
8. List skills with proficiency levels
9. Keep formatting clean and professional
10. Tailor content to specific job requirements
